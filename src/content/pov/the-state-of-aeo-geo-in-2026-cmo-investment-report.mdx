## The news

Conductor released its 2026 State of AEO/GEO report, surveying CMOs on how they're allocating budget toward AI-powered search experiences — answer engine optimization (AEO) and generative engine optimization (GEO). The data shows enterprise brands are actively reallocating resources toward these channels as customers increasingly start their purchase journeys inside AI tools rather than traditional search.

## My take

The report is right that something real is happening. When customers ask ChatGPT or Perplexity which brand to buy, and your brand isn't in the answer, you've been disintermediated before the consideration even started. That's not a traffic problem. That's a demand creation problem.

But here's where I'd push back on how most teams will respond: GEO is not SEO with a new acronym. The game in traditional search was largely technical — structure your content right, earn the right backlinks, rank for the right terms. AI-generated responses are curated differently. They favor brands with clear positioning, consistent messaging, and genuine authority on a topic. That's brand work. That's content with a point of view. That's the kind of marketing that compounds over time.

CMOs who treat GEO as a channel optimization project and hand it to the SEO team will get mediocre results. The brands that will show up in AI-generated answers are the ones that have done the hard work of being clearly, consistently, and specifically about something. If your brand positioning is vague, no amount of technical optimization will save you.

There's also a consumer insight angle that the report doesn't address: we don't actually know yet how much customers trust AI-generated recommendations, or how that trust varies by category. Buying a software tool after an AI recommendation is different from choosing a medical device or a baby formula. Before over-rotating budget, the most important thing a CMO can do is understand where their customer's journey actually starts — and how much of that is shifting to AI channels in their specific category.

## The so-what

Audit your brand positioning before you audit your GEO strategy. If you can't articulate what your brand clearly owns — and back it with consistent content across your channels — no technical optimization will get you into the answer. The CMOs who will win in AI search are the ones who already built brands worth mentioning. If your positioning is fuzzy, that's the problem to solve first.
